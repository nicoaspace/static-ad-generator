## BRAND OVERVIEW

**Name:** LMNT (pronounced "element")
**Tagline:** "Zero Sugar Electrolytes" / "Stay Salty" / "More Salt, Not Less"
**Sub-tagline:** "Paleo-Keto Friendly Hydration"
**Design Agency:** In-house / no publicly credited agency (brand built by co-founders James Murphy, Robb Wolf, Luis Villaseñor, Nicki Violetti, Tyler Cartwright)
**Founded:** 2018

**Voice Adjectives [5]:**
1. **Blunt** — speaks plainly, no hedging ("More Salt, Not Less")
2. **Scientific** — backs every claim with research, references sodium ratios, biochemistry
3. **Rebellious** — frames the brand as a "Salty Rebellion" against the sugar-packed hydration status quo
4. **Confident** — never apologetic about high sodium content; leans into what others avoid
5. **Warm-casual** — locker-room camaraderie ("Stay Salty," "Our Salty Squad," "Salt Dealer")

**Mission:** "We're on a mission to restore health through hydration."

**Positioning:**
Premium, zero-sugar, zero-filler electrolyte drink mix for athletes, keto/paleo/fasting practitioners, and health-conscious performers who need serious hydration without garbage ingredients. Highest sodium formula on the market: 1,000mg sodium + 200mg potassium + 60mg magnesium per packet.

**Competitive Differentiation:**
| | LMNT | Liquid IV | Nuun |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sodium | 1,000mg | ~500mg | ~300mg |
| Sugar | Zero | Yes | Minimal |
| Price/packet | ~$1.50 | ~$1.50 | ~$0.70 |
| Audience | Keto/paleo/athletes/fasting | General hydration | Casual gym |
| Brand angle | Salt-first rebellion, science | Flavor variety, convenience | Eco-friendly, tablets |

LMNT's key visual differentiator vs. competitors: bold black packaging with vivid flavor-coded color accents, vs. Liquid IV's white/pastel packaging and Nuun's tablet tubes. LMNT looks more like a performance supplement than a lifestyle drink.

## VISUAL SYSTEM

**Primary Font:** Bebas Neue — ultra-condensed, all-caps, heavy weight. Used for hero headlines, product name lockup, and bold ad copy overlays. Very tight letter-spacing, no descenders. Classic performance/supplement aesthetic.

**Secondary Font:** Graphik — modern geometric sans-serif. Used for body copy, product descriptions, ingredient callouts, and sub-headlines. Clean, legible at small sizes. Regular and medium weights.

**Primary Color:** Black `#0A0A0A` — dominant packaging background; creates premium, high-contrast feel

**Secondary Color:** White `#FFFFFF` — all reversed-out text and logo on black backgrounds; primary body copy color on dark packaging

**Accent / CTA Color:** Warm Red `#D91E18` — primary action color on website (buy buttons, CTAs, highlights)

**Background Colors:** Matte black (packaging), clean white (web editorial sections), off-white cream (science/educational content areas)

**CTA Color and Style:** Solid red `#D91E18` rectangular buttons with white all-caps Graphik text. No border-radius, no drop shadow — flat, blunt, performance-brand aesthetic.

## PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION

**Lighting:** Two distinct modes:
1. **Hero product shots** — High-key, clean studio lighting. Bright, even illumination, minimal shadows. White or very light neutral backgrounds. Product is the unambiguous hero.
2. **Lifestyle/performance shots** — Natural light with slight warmth; outdoor environments (desert, gym, trail). Exposure leans bright to maintain clean feel. Occasional dramatic rim lighting for athletic scenes.

**Color Grading:** Punchy and saturated, not filmic or muted. Product colors (yellows, pinks, oranges) pop against dark or neutral backgrounds. Blacks are true black. No vintage washes or desaturation.

**Composition:** Product-forward. Either centered product on flat lay with minimal props, or product integrated into an active scene (hand holding stick pack, glass of dissolved drink in foreground with athlete blurred behind). Rule-of-thirds used rarely — LMNT favors direct center framing for product.

**Subject Matter:**
- 30-count black box displayed front-facing
- Individual stick packs fanned out or stacked
- Clear glass/shaker bottle with dissolved drink (showing flavor color)
- Active human subjects: runners, cyclists, CrossFit athletes, hikers
- Founder Robb Wolf appears in editorial/educational content
- Hands opening a stick pack (UGC-style authenticity)

**Props and Surfaces:**
- Minimal props: water bottle, clear glass, gym towel, kettlebell
- Surfaces: concrete, slate, black matte surface, gym floor, sandy/desert terrain
- No excess lifestyle clutter — everything in frame serves a function

**Mood:** No-nonsense, direct, slightly edgy. Performance brand, not wellness spa. Feels more CrossFit box than yoga studio. Empowering rather than soothing.

## PRODUCT DETAILS

**Physical Description:**
LMNT Recharge comes in two primary formats:
1. **30-Count Box** — Rectangular box, approximately 5.5" × 5.5" × 2.5". Matte black exterior with flavor-specific color accent on front panel. Box opens with a custom diecut slit closure (closes like a purse). Interior printed black with "Stay Salty" in gray.
2. **Individual Stick Packs** — Single-serve powder sachets (~4g), approximately 1" × 5". Color-coded per flavor. Matte finish. "LMNT" wordmark and flavor name on front.

**Label/Logo Placement:**
- "LMNT" wordmark in bold condensed all-caps, centered top-third of packaging face
- Flavor name below in same typeface, slightly smaller, in the flavor's accent color
- "Zero Sugar Electrolytes" in smaller Graphik below flavor name
- Electrolyte ratios (1000mg Na / 200mg K / 60mg Mg) may appear on side panel
- Nutrition facts panel on back

**Distinctive Features:**
- Black-dominant packaging stands out in supplement/health aisles (most competitors use white/light packaging)
- Spot UV gloss on packet photos on the box exterior — tactile premium finish
- Spot UV on "Stay Salty" text — brand catchphrase reinforced as premium detail
- Color-coded flavor system allows instant flavor recognition in mixed-flavor contexts
- Corrugated cardboard (not soft pouch) — feels more like a supplement brand than a beverage brand

**Packaging System:**
- 30-count single flavor boxes (hero SKU)
- 12-count variety packs
- Sampler packs (8-count variety)
- INSIDER subscription boxes
- Each SKU maintains the same black-box / color-accent / Bebas Neue typography system

## AD CREATIVE STYLE

**Typical Formats:**
- Static product hero (box centered, black or gradient background, bold copy overlay)
- Flat lay (stick packs arranged on slate/concrete surface, overhead shot)
- Before/after energy/cramp/fatigue testimonial cards (dark background, white text)
- Science stat callouts ("1,000mg Sodium per packet. No sugar. No BS.")
- UGC-style athlete or lifestyle photo with text overlay
- Side-by-side comparison (LMNT vs. competitors — sodium content highlighted)
- Founder content featuring Robb Wolf with quote overlays
- Subscription/offer ads ("Subscribe & Save 13%" on INSIDER program)

**Text Overlay Style:**
- Bebas Neue all-caps for primary headline — large, fills width of frame
- Graphik regular for supporting copy — 2–3 lines max
- High contrast: white text on black/dark, or black text on light
- Minimal text — trust the product. Usually 1 bold claim + 1 supporting line + CTA
- No decorative flourishes; no script fonts; no icons except occasionally a simple divider line

**Photo vs. Illustration:**
Overwhelmingly photo-based. Clean product photography + lifestyle photography. No illustration except rare educational infographics (electrolyte ratio charts, body diagrams in science content). Brand is built on credibility — illustration would undermine the "science-backed" positioning.

**UGC Usage:**
High. Community-driven brand with strong keto/paleo/CrossFit base. UGC shows real athletes, real sweat, real fatigue/recovery. Formatted as screenshot-style testimonials with user handle, or raw phone footage edited with text overlays. "Stay Salty" community hashtag usage.

**Offer Presentation:**
Subscription (INSIDER program) framed as the "smart choice" — "Best value" badge, savings percentage prominently displayed. Trial variety packs (8-pack, 12-pack) used as low-commitment acquisition offers. No heavy discounting — premium pricing held with "try before you commit" framing.
